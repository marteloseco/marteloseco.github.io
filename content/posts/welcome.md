## A Different Kind of Forge

Five years ago, I was a blacksmith. Literal hammer and anvil, shaping metal in a workshop. But I saw the writing on the wall - the future wasn't in horseshoes and wrought iron gates. So I taught myself to code and pivoted to software.

Turns out hammering out code isn't that different from hammering metal. You heat things up, shape them until they work, and sometimes you have to start over when things break. The main difference? My fingers don't get burned as often now.

## Why I Started This Blog

2025 is the year of opportunity. With LLMs becoming more accessible, there's never been a better time to build and scale software businesses. I'm here to share what I'm learning as I ship fast and iterate faster.

The blacksmith mindset still applies: strike while the iron is hot, don't overthink the design, and sometimes the best way forward is just to hit it harder.
